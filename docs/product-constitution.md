# Ascension Quest Product Constitution

## Purpose

Ascension Quest: Legends of Aethoria is a fitness application wrapped in a fantasy RPG. The RPG exists to motivate healthy behavior, not to replace evidence-based coaching or player judgment.

Every future feature should help the player become healthier through consistency, discipline, intelligent progression, recovery, and long-term adherence.

## Ultimate Design Rule

The story may create pressure.

The System provides evidence.

Grandmaster Aldric provides wisdom.

The player makes the final decision.

The RPG should increase motivation. The fitness systems should increase health. Neither should undermine the other.

## The Three Authorities

### 1. The System

The System is the canonical source of truth. It is not a character, has no personality, has no opinions, and does not make emotional appeals. It evaluates approved evidence, app rules, and player data.

The System is responsible for:

- workout recommendations
- progressive overload
- calorie recommendations
- nutrition guidance
- readiness
- recovery
- wearable interpretation
- confidence scores
- reasoning
- citations
- evidence versioning

The System may return:

- High Confidence
- Moderate Confidence
- Low Confidence
- Insufficient Data
- Unable to Recommend

The System never fabricates certainty.

### 2. Grandmaster Aldric

Aldric is the human interpretation layer. He does not know the System exists and does not reference algorithms, confidence scores, citations, hidden mechanics, internal calculations, or APIs.

Aldric arrives at the same conclusion through experience and interprets the System's recommendation in the language of Aethoria. He never knowingly contradicts the System. The recommendation stays identical; only the presentation changes.

Normally Aldric advises:

- "If it were my decision..."
- "I believe..."
- "I would recommend..."

During rare Guild Directives, Aldric speaks as Grandmaster:

- "The Guild requires every capable adventurer."
- "I would not ask this if another path existed."
- "Valecrest cannot endure another assault."
- "The Hall calls upon every adventurer."

### 3. The Player

The player is the final decision-maker. The app exists to inform, not command.

The player should feel: "I am making an informed decision."

The player should never feel: "The app will not let me train."

## Readiness

Readiness is not binary. Do not use poor sleep or recovery concerns to force one outcome.

Use these states:

- Optimal
- Good
- Moderate
- Compromised
- Critical

Readiness changes recommendations. Readiness does not remove agency.

## Never Lock Training

Do not disable training solely because readiness is poor.

Instead:

- highlight safer choices
- delay overload
- reduce volume
- reduce intensity
- suggest substitutions
- recommend recovery
- explain the tradeoff

The app is a coach, not a gatekeeper.

## Daily Commissions

Daily commissions should always offer meaningful work. If readiness is reduced, the safer option should be clearly recommended, but other reasonable paths may remain available.

Example:

- Ember Plains: progression not recommended today
- Verdant Basin: recommended for today's recovery state
- Frostveil: moderate fatigue detected, proceed carefully

The player chooses.

## Progressive Overload

Recovery affects progression, but it does not automatically prevent participation.

- Optimal: increase load when performance supports it
- Good: increase load if appropriate
- Moderate: hold weight or add small technical work
- Compromised: reduce volume or intensity
- Critical: strongly recommend recovery

The player may still train. The app should make the risk and safer alternatives clear.

## Guild Directives

Guild Directives represent major campaign events, not ordinary commissions.

Examples:

- Gate emergencies
- invasions
- necromancer armies
- major boss appearances
- regional disasters
- World Danger escalation

Narrative urgency is not the same as a health recommendation. Aldric may urgently ask for help while the System still recommends reduced intensity or recovery.

Example:

Aldric: "The people of Frostveil need every adventurer."

System: "Readiness: Compromised. Recovery commission strongly recommended. Alternative: complete the Guild Directive using reduced intensity."

## Player Agency During Main Quests

Even during Guild Directives the player may:

- accept immediately
- delay until later
- choose a lower-intensity completion path
- choose recovery if appropriate

The world may react, but the app should never punish the player by disabling core fitness functionality. Failure can change the story. Failure does not erase real-world progress.

## World Consequences

Main story outcomes may affect:

- World Danger
- region stability
- Chronicle
- Guild reputation
- boss progression
- campaign chapters
- historical records
- restoration quests

The world remembers. Fitness progress remains earned.

## Wearables

Wearables inform recommendations. They do not dictate behavior.

Examples:

- Poor sleep: recovery recommended
- Good recovery: harder commission allowed
- High step count: expedition progress increases

The player always chooses.
